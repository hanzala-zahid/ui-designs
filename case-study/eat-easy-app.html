Eat Easy App Design

Eat Easy Mobile app UI/UX Case study

About Project

Through efficient and enticing designing strategies, UIDesignz's UI/UX services assist brands in developing identities that are engaging, enjoyable, and trustworthy. Our user interface design services help new brands to reach their audience and leave a positive, long-lasting impression.

Introduction

For us to be healthy, we need to eat. And for some of us, eating serves purposes other than simply satisfying our hunger. It's the basic need of our body. Our UIDesignz's UI/UX services assist our customers who want to design food-based apps for their consumers. In this case study, we will describe our whole experience of how we created a food delivery app for restaurants from scratch to its final stage.

The Problem

People struggle to find the time to prepare meals at home in the chaos of today's fast-paced society. Some people simply lack time to eat out. Finding a method for these people to order and get food at their convenience is a big problem.

The Objective

Our objective was to create a smartphone app enabling users to quickly and conveniently order meals from restaurants and food stores. Our responsibility: We are the app's designer and must design the app from scratch to its final stage based on our customer's needs.

Analyzing The User's Goals

Analyzing if the platform is easy to use for primary users. Analyzing if users want to pay after delivery or before it. Providing different languages for easy understanding. Easy tracking system.
Access to menus of various restaurants and price comparison

Analyzing Different Food Apps

Before developing my solution, we looked at a few well-known apps for meal delivery. We engaged with the platforms and analyzed the client feedback. This assisted me in discovering some issues with the current solutions and potential improvements to the user experience. Access to menus of various restaurants and price comparison

Developing Stage

User flow

We mapped a user flow that prioritized recognition over recall to accomplish my objective of producing an intuitive design. This was accomplished by designing a recognizable by the users.

Paper Designs

After mapping out the user flow, we started drawing designs. To ensure that each component's components are in the right place for ease of access, our team designed the first page based on the user's accessibility analysis and market research that will help users to order quickly without any hassle.

Shaping the Designs

Our team designed everything based on users' feedback and market research, and then we used programming software to shape our designs.

Conclusion

Consideration of the response from the users highlighted a need for information supply for consumers in the eating-out setting, including the opportunity to limit the information offered to that which was individually relevant or exciting. The app's goal is to inform users about the food options while giving them the opportunity to choose whatever they want. Feel free to contact our team
