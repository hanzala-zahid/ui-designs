A UX Case Study on Creating
a Studious Environment for Students

Andrew Jonson Posted on 27th January 2021

User Research

What Motivates Students to Study?
We began by attempting to comprehend the kids' intentions. We kept the following crucial questions in mind: -Why do pupils work so hard in school? -What do students want to gain from their studies? -What does studying entail for students? We attempted to find answers to those issues through our research (Zoom interviews, reading threads, and watching study-related YouTube videos). We discovered the following significant findings: -Students desire to accomplish their tasks quickly to graduate and acquire their degrees. -Students want to achieve well in school and be proud of their accomplishments.
In short, pupils can view studying as a measure of success.

What is interfering with their studies?
While we recognized their primary purpose for learning, we still wanted to delve deeper and ask why some students could not stay focused. The essential questions we now kept in mind, similar to our first approach, were:
-What prevents students from studying?

-What motivates students to continue their studies?

-When do students believe they need to start studying?
While there are several distractions in our environment, many participants stated that their smartphones were their primary source of distraction. Their phones' innumerable apps (for example, Instagram and mobile gaming) hindered them from being productive.

Takeaways from Research

According to our analysis, the following features must be incorporated into our service: -Reduce smartphone distractions -Create a "co-study" environment -Create a "co-study" environment -Enter their goal(s) as they begin their studies
